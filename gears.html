Gears

Some gear that I have and typically use.

Daily Gear

Hoodie

I am basically wearing a hoodie all the time. I am not a fan of sun directly on my skin, it feels warm compared to the additional temp from the extra hoodie layer. I also usually get that kangaroo pocket on the front for bonus holding stuff which can be helpful.

Baseball hat

Hats are something that I like and my go to's are my Mariners hat, and my Varusteleka velcro hat. Where and what I'm going to do determines which I'm going to wear. The brim is very helpful to keep sun out of my eyes and provide minimal protection from things hitting your face.

Moto G5

This is the current phone I have. It's a piece of shit, but it gets all the stuff I want done with it done. I'm currently using ParanoidOS rather than the version of Android that came on it because Motorola had some dumb bloat ware on it and was less customizable.

Ticwatch E

This is my smart watch. It's pretty much only useful to me as a fitness tracker, notification reader, timer/stop watch, and normal watch. If you want this watch I would advise getting a better band than the rubber one that comes with it because that one is kinda fragile.

Atlas Ankle Med Kit

This is my EDC med kit, in it I have chest seals, QuikClot gauze, medical shears, and a CAT-7 Tourniquet. I would recommend that everyone have some kind of medical training and carry medical gear with them. It doesn't need to be nearly as trauma focused as this, but some kind of medical gear is very rarely a bad thing.

Shooting Gear

WWSD AR-15

This is my AR based on the What Would Stoner Do project from InRangeTv. I have an SR-15 lower, Aero Precision upper, SLT-2 Trigger, Faxon 16" Pencil Barrel, ALG 13" hand guard, and a red dot. Very boring, basic, cheap, and light AR.

Beretta 92 GD

My usual hand gun for competition. I have changed this to use the G style decocker only lever rather than the safety decocker. I also replaced the hammer spring with the D spring to improve the trigger.

PF940c

Why buy a Glock when you can make your own? Well the Glock probably works more consistently, or at least more consistently than my P80... Whatever this is my striker fire gun. It still needs some more rounds through it before I want to bring it to competition. It's not quite smooth at the moment.

Safariland ALS 92 Holster

This is the holster I go to when I need pistol retention. It was given to me by a fellow DerpGru member during the 2019 spring Covert Cthulhu event. Thanks man!

Eclipse 92 Holster

This is the holster I go to for fast draws and events that I don't need to be running jumping and rolling around.

Eclipse P80 Holster

This is the holster I go to when I want to shoot my P80. It doesn't fit as well as the same brand & model holster on my 92.

5.11 Double Stack Pistol Mag Pouch

This is literally the 2nd piece of gear that I bought. They work, no they are not super speedy race gear, but they actually hold onto your mags while you do dumb shit like run and jump and roll around even without the retention Velcro which is redundant in my experience.

EssTac KYWI Triple Mid

Wow look they go on my plate carrier and hold 3 PMags... They do the job, they hold the mags, they are durable. As far as I can tell it's either these or HSGI Tacos for pouches that stay open without a mag in them. If you have a different piece of gear send me an email and I will look into them.

Crye JPC 2.0

CryE oR DiE. This is the carrier that I chose because I'm a small guy and Crye happens to offer a carrier that fits SAPI Small plates and has a few extra bits, mostly the zip on back plate things, that are more attractive than the other carriers. If someone wants to make me a SAPI Extra Small carrier PLEASE contact me because the only one I found is over priced as fuck. Me@G3yost.com

Level IV Armor

I have level IV SAPI Small plates (8.75" x 11.75"), I got mine from Tri State Armor. For those of you who are "non standard sizes" either tiny or huge he has SAPI Extra Small - SAPI Extra Large. This guy was very accommodating when there were some order glitches due to him swapping his website system near my time of purchase. These plates weigh !!!WIGHT IN LBS & KG!!! which... sucks but is better than it could be I guess.

Computer Gear

MU-TH-UR

This is my desktop. Right now it's only Windows 10... I really like Linux & 7 and have done what I can to revert it back to a 7 style. I also have the Linux Subsystem for Windows, yeah it's not actually Linux, but it lets me use stuff like GCC, youtube-dl, and other command line tools that should never be put through the torture of Windows command prompt.

LV-426

This is my laptop. Its and old beat to shit Asus GL-551 that has Linux Mint & Windows 10 on it. The Windows partition is the box that i abuse with bad bloat ware that I don't want to pollute MU-TH-UR. Typically however it is booted into Mint because it is way more efficient and more useful for it's purpose.

VoiceMeeter Potato

The best tool I know of for managing multiple audio streams on one computer. There are three levels of VoiceMeeter and Potato is the most complex. Potato also has the capacity to be a sound board. Very much worth having in games and discord servers.

Wallpaper Engine

Want a cool custom moving and possibly interactive wallpaper for your Windows desktop? Go get Wallpaper Engine and combine it with Rainmeter for a pretty slick looking desktop.

Rainmeter

A tool for interactive desktop that pairs very well with Wallpaper Engine to make my desktop.

Classic Shell

Do you still want updates from Microsoft, so you have Windows 10 installed, but you hate the aesthetics and function of parts? No problem go get Classic Shell. I only really use the start menu portion, but there are some pretty cool tools hidden in there.

Clover

Do you like Chineseium? Do you also want to cram some of that shit into the windows file explorer? Well I have and it's pretty slick. Clover adds chrome style tabs to explorer. Very useful for me.

Sublime Text 3

My plain text editor. It's got some syntax highlighting, but the multi-cursor function is the real reason why I like Sublime.

Ergodox

The keyboard is the most useful tool I have for dealing with my computer, so rather than put up with the ones you can buy I made my own. Mainly with Cherry MX Blues, I also have Clears and Browns on specific keys. The reprogramability and robust nature of key combinations that can be made using just the EZ Configurator is insane, and that is without any tweaking in QMK or TMK.

Razer Mamba 2012

Wires are annoying when you wanna whip your hand across your desk quickly and the best version of a wireless mouse that existed at the time was the Mamba 2012. If I had one thing to improve it would be Razer Synapse. Razer should stick to hardware design and outsource EVERY SINGLE LINE OF CODE to somewhere else.

Elecom M-XT3DRBK

I really like track balls for laptop use and desktop browsing. I started out with the Logitech m570 and eventually wore out the buttons like many users of those mice do. I then stumbled upon what I would consider to be a strict upgrade and a much better design. I have broken one however because I dropped it and it then got run over by a van when I was on my way to a friends how to do a group project.

Audio-Technica ATX M40x

A pretty decent set of headphones that I use when gaming. Go listen to some headphones with the same source and pick the ones you want. I bought these for gaming, not for music so the audio curve is pretty flat.

AKG M220

These are the headphones I have mounted to my Vive. They are open back so that I can give clear instructions to the person that is playing and they can still hear the game. Hey they were like $45 on Massdrop at the time so I thought what the hell.

Vive

I picked up my Vive used for 500 bucks in 2017 so that I could play Pavlov and Beat Saber. It's a good time if you have the space to flail your arms and be a derp. I would recommend at least trying VR once, however having a good guide is very helpful for a good time. Solid introductory games include: The Lab, Beat Saber, and Tilt Brush.
